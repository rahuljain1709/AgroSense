Pomegranate is a hardy fruit crop well-suited to dry and semi-arid regions. 
It grows best in well-drained loamy or sandy loam soils. 
Pomegranate trees can tolerate drought but respond well to regular irrigation during fruit development. 
The crop requires balanced fertilization and is sensitive to micronutrient deficiencies. 
Proper pruning ensures good canopy structure and fruit quality. 
Cracking of fruits can occur under irregular irrigation.
